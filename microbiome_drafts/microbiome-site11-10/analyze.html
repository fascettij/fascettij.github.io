Analyzing a Microbiome

How do you analyze a microbiome?

Microbiomes are composed of many different species of microorganisms, and each microbiome (both microbiomes intra- and inter-person) can be vastly different from one another. Thus, being able to determine what an individualâ€™s microbiome is made up of is very important. To do this, the genetic code of the microbes can be sequenced. From a sample, scientists can extract the genetic code and then sequence it to know what the DNA is. Typically, researchers that want some microbiome samples sequenced will pay a third party company, send the samples to be sequenced, and receive electronic files with the DNA code from the sample on it. There are two main ways to sequence a microbiomic sample: 16S rRNA and Shotgun Metagenomics. Most of the time, researchers must choose one method over the other for budget reasons, but there are pros and cons to each one.
